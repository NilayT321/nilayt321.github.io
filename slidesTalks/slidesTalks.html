This pages contains some of the various (non-research) talks and slides that I have done.
I gave the following talk after a semester-long independent study (which extends my DRP from September 2024).

  • Integrable Systems in Hamiltonian Dynamics (May 2025)

    Abstract: Roughly speaking, integrable systems are dynamical systems which have many conserved quantities. After reviewing the basics, we will then prove the Liouville–Arnold theorem, which reveals a surprising topological connection regarding some properties of these dynamical systems.

The RUMA seminar talks are a series of talks delivered by undergraduate students and targeted towards an undergraduate audience. I delivered the following talk as part of the RUMA seminar talks:

  • Introduction to Homology Groups & Applications (Novemeber 2024)

    Abstract: Algebraic topology is a field of mathematics that studies topological spaces through algebraic invariants. In this talk, we discuss homology groups, one of the most widely studied and important topics in modern topology. We will give an introduction to homology groups, some elementary computations, and some applications to see what we can learn from them.

In the DRP program, an undergraduate mentee is paired with a graduate student mentor to read and present on a topic of interest. I participated in the DRP twice and delivered the following presentations:

  • Smooth Manifolds & Symplectic Geometry (September 2024) Slides

    Abstract: We start by giving a brief overview of topological manifolds, smooth structures on manifolds, and differential forms. We then discuss the basic concepts and definitions regarding symplectic manifolds and some brief applications in Hamiltonian mechanics.
